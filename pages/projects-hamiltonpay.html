HamiltonPay

Online payment platform for micro-entrepreneurs

Project: FinTech • Responsive Web
Scope: Strategy • Research • Design • Copy Writing & Editing
Roles:
  • Designed responsive dashboards & landing pages for multiple products
  • Adapted & maintained a customized designed system in Figma
  • Wrote & edited website copy, video demo scripts, and pitch deck content
  • Worked closely with dev team to quickly build multiple functional product demos
Tools: Figma • Tailwind • Miro • Canva
Year: 2021-2022

Enabling faster, safer, cheaper transactions

HamiltonPay is an improved payment collection platform that embraces smart contracts to reduce costs stemming gtom fraud, labor, and human error. These costs disproportionately affect micro-entrepreneurs who sell goods and services.

I designed user-friendly payment flows, dashboards, and integration flows for six high-fidelity, functioning product demos, attracting a range of early adopters and investors.

My Contributions

▶ Designed custom variations of a product

I worked closely with the development team to understand the back-end well enough to design landing pages, dashboard views, and key user flows specific to:

  • Individual fitness trainers, and their students and customers
  • Independent health practitioners, and their patients
  • Micro entrepreneurs, and their buyers and customers
  • Web developers building sites using HamiltonPayâ€™s API


One challenge was designing the consumer-side interface. These were end-users who had not chosen to pay using the app. To communicate trust, I crafted transparent explanations. To balance trust and ease of use, I designed the UI to pair automated steps with the equivalent manual option.

▶ Explored screens for tie-in of third-party integrations

Tying in third-party integrations often leads to confusion as the end-user moves between different environments. The developers wanted integrations as hold-overs until they could be built in-house. I explored how they could initially plug in seamlessly to user flows for initial demos, and for validation for future scope.

▶ Established a preliminary design system

Using Tailwind UI components, I customized and managed a preliminary design system in Figma. The resulting layouts were essentially wireframes with the appearance of finished products, that could be coded at speed. This allowed me to user-test work flows without the need to link together pixel-perfect prototype screens, before nailing down a final design.

Takeaways

Being a solo designer on a developer-led team

  • A developer-created admin view was a great starting point understand what data is truly required to accomplish a given task. This allowed me to streamline what information I asked customers to provide.
  • By consistently emphasizing product usability, I was able to remind the team how UX design is foundational to the products, not simply a cosmetic overlay.
  • It was a chance to wear many hats: I served as copy editor, contributed to marketing material, and did the voiceovers for demo videos.
