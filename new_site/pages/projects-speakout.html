SpeakOut

Student portal for affordable, high-quality English classes in Bahrain

Work completed for SpeakOut in partnership with UX course.

Overcoming economic barriers

While English is the language of commerce, government, and higher education in Bahrain, students can rarely gain functional proficiency from public school alone. SpeakOut provides low-cost, high-quality courses, taught by well-trained instructors using interactive modalities to propel students to mastery.

To pay teachers fairly, support roles were bootstrapped. The school faced two critical pain-points: much-needed revenue lost due to unpaid tuition, and lack of registration follow-through due to missed placement test appointments. A solution was urgently needed as the school expanded to a second location.

Our team conducted a two-week design sprint with the school’s founder. We workshopped scope, conducted background research, and then divided design work. I envisioned a new online registration flow and tested concepts on proxy-users. Finally, we presented findings, feature roll-out recommendations, and schematic designs for key screens based on proxy-user feedback.

My Contributions

Week 1: Scoping & Research

The team and SpeakOut’s founder first identified the school’s biggest need: broadly, to reduce educators’ administrative workload without increasing overhead. We identified online registration as highest priority, since it would establish the student database to support most other improvements.

A website audit and journey mapping exercise revealed that the landing page, fees, and course info screens could use clarification in support of making registration more inviting.

Week 1 toolbox included: stakeholder interviews, online research for context, comparative analysis, dot voting / polls, existing workflow audits, personas, journey mapping, card sorting, key user flows, crazy-8s, 4-step sketches

Week 2: Design & Testing

With multiple designers on the team, we divided tasks. I focused on the registration flow, while others worked on the style guide, website restructuring, and static screens (landing page, class schedule, fees).

Starting with diagrams, I could quickly get feedback from the founder. I realized that registration would also require a mobile payment integration and a sign-in flow.

By sharing a wireframe prototype, I learned that parents typically registered multiple children -- even teens. Creating household registration was unavoidable if we were going to propose a realistic solution.

Recommended future improvements

For our final day of the sprint, we demoed a low-fi prototype with a few key screens styled. We proposed a website redesign with added registration, sign-in, and payment features.

Our additional recommendations, in order of priority:

  • Toggle feature for Arabic (right-to-left text) versions of all screens
  • Ability to take multiple choice portion of placement test online
  • Calendar scheduling for oral placement tests
  • Mobile notifications / reminders for payment and appointments
  • An administrator-side dashboard
  • Digital records access (grades, certificates, etc.) in Student Portals

Takeaways

The design sprint experience

  • Scope creep was a constant threat -- no one on the design team had participated in a sprint. It helped to review a brief PRD each morning for a group reality-check.
  • Ideally, participants would have included at least one teacher, a parent, and a student from the SpeakOut community.
